31 Days of Punk

The punk album for 12/02/2025 is:

Static Age

by The Misfits

Ben's thoughts

The word “Punk” loses part of its meaning without mentioning The Misfits, a band whose impact on the genre far exceeds most of their colleagues. Their album “Static Age” recorded in 1978 is a rocket of fast paced, chest pounding, politically contemporary lyrics. And while the album was recorded within 30 hours of studio time, none of it feels rushed. In true Misfits fashion, Glenn Danzig never stops belting for all 17 tracks, which oddly enough makes many of the transitions seamless throughout the entire listening experience. One of the singles for the album "Bullet” takes us through the Kennedy assassination. A frightening, rageful outburst reminiscent of the public's feelings towards political violence at the time, especially when it came to one of the most progressive presidents of the 20th century. The line “Texas is the reason that the president’s dead” rings out like a call to action. The resounding theme of the album is our human drive towards sex. Encased not only in passionate emotion, but also in power, abuse, and hatred. “Hollywood Babylon” asks the listener if the citizens kneel for sex, and "Teenagers from Mars” tells the story of a couple of extraterrestrial adolescents going on an invasive rampage without need for justification, as they view the Earth's people as mindless and meaningless.

The Misfits deliver some of the best tracks of their discography with this release. It is no surprise that this band, and album, are etched deep into the punk hall of fame, inspiring countless artists and bands for decades to come. If you want to feel that classic, loud, and angry music that pioneered the punk genre, look no further than Static Age.

Connor's thoughts

Gross, violent, heavy. The three words I would choose to describe Static Age by The Misfits. With lyrics like “The insemination of little girls in the middle of wet dreams”, it’s hard to not feel uncomfortable listening to The Misfits discography, but that’s exactly what makes them one of the most unique bands of all time.

Recorded in 30 hours, Static Age is filled with some of The Misfits greatest hits like ‘Last Caress’, ‘She’, and ‘Angelfuck’ which utilize controversial and uncomfortable lyrics to enthrall listeners into their sub-genre, Horror-punk. They rip through 17 tracks of brain-throbbing bass lines to support the heavy nature of the band, and treat each song like a sprint to scream about the most contentious topics the late 70s had to offer. It’s also worth mentioning that for an album recorded in the 70s and released in the 90s, the content of their songs is unsurprisingly contemporary, supporting the many reasons they continue to captivate younger listeners.

From my first listen of ‘Bullet’ to my obsession with the inner turmoil of Glenn Danzig, the band, and their audience, I don’t know if there is a more distinctive band I've listened to in my life, and as far as my Mt. Rushmore of punk goes, The Misfits take the first spot.

The shortest-longest album of this month, and possibly my favorite, Static Age is the perfect entry into the grotesque world of The Misfits, and I could not recommend it more.

What is 31 Days of Punk?

The dictionary defines Punk Rock as,

"A loud, fast-moving, and aggressive form of rock music, popular in the late 1970s and early 1980s."

But punk music is so much more than what a dictionary says it is. In fact, the rebellious nature of punk rock would probably argue against a definition at all. Punk rock took the rock and roll of the 50s and 60s, and said "How do we break the rules", to which broken voices, fast tempos, and dirty guitars responded as the spark that ignited a new genre of music, and quickly became the arbiter of counterculture for generations to come.

31 Days of Punk is a celebration of punk music for the entire month of December. Each day, we will introduce a new punk album spanning from its roots in the 60s, to the noisy, coming of age pop-punk of the 90s, to contemporary punk that rocks the concert halls of today. There will be no repeating artists, and to try and cover every corner of this massive genre, each entry will have a category tied to it, including International, Ska-punk, Afro-punk and many more! We will share our thoughts on each album, from the historical details to the general reasons why we love them, and more about how these albums have shaped our listening experiences through the years.

The intention of this blog is not to critique or rank these albums, but rather to celebrate and elevate the music that we love so much. For anyone who is unfamiliar with this genre, this blog may also act as a blueprint of great albums to start with.

We hope you enjoy this as much as we do!
